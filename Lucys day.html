St. Lucy's Day!

St. Lucy's Day, celebrated on December 13, originates from Scandinavian and Italian traditions honoring Saint Lucia, a Christian martyr.

This holiday marks the beginning of the Christmas season and symbolizes light in the darkness of winter.

Traditional activities include parades and the singing of carols, where girls often dress in white robes with red sashes, wearing wreaths adorned with candles on their heads.

Typical foods include saffron buns known as "lussekatter" and various festive pastries.
